What it’s all about

For five years the Open Knowledge Foundation Germany has been advocating open knowledge, open data, transparency, and civil participation. Since then many applications that make the world a little bit better have been developed.

Without the contribution of our community this wouldn't have been possible. That’s why we want to celebrate with you and invite everyone who wants to advance, think further and get to know open data in Germany.

We offer dataenthusiasts, political activists, non-profit organisations, entrepreneurs and representatives from politics and public administrations a colourful programme with keynotes and project pitches, barcamps and workshops and several surprises.

More to come soon in our programme


Hard facts

The Datensummit takes place on friday the 28th and saturday the 29th of April in Berlin.

2 days, 2 venues. On the first day we are being hosted by the German Federal Ministry of Transport and Digital Infrastructure – BMVI, with many international speakers attending. On the second day we will collect and merge all the new ideas and develop plans, demands and open data projects in barcamps and workshops.

No Techie? No Problem!

A software, a data visualisation or platform is not an end in itself. Data can help understand and overcome many societal challenges, such as it environmental pollution, traffic congestion or the outcome of political elections.

To use data for political and social matters, there is no need for an IT degree. Much can be realized with simple measures. How to find and prepare data, and tell a story with it, can be learned on site in our workshops or the barcamp.



Who we are

The festival is organised by the Open Knowledge Foundation Germany. Over the past five years we have built a extensive community, which we want to unite at the Datensummit. Especially the individual communities involved in Datenschule and Code for Germany.

Datenschule supports non-profit organisations to reach their societal goals by offering training in working with data. Code for Germany brings together developers, designers and those interested in open data in so called OK Labs - engaged citizens voluntarily working on tools to improve society - present in 25 Germany cities.

The festival is funded by the German Federal Ministry of Transport and Digital Infrastructure.
